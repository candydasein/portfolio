Michael Loren Siegel

Full Stack Web Developer

I am a full stack web developer with a background in higher education, strategic communications, and social entrepreneurship. I love nothing more than solving problems, learning and growing, connecting people to one another, and helping others make their lives more fulfilling and whole.

I have 17 years of professional experience to date. I have previously worked as a professor and scholar of film and digital media studies, a higher ed communications strategist and institutional reformer in the area of diversity, inclusion, and equity, and a writer, editor and translator. I am, in addition, an officially certified Tea Master, and I own a small tea import and sales business called Three Mouths Tea which supports the autonomy of tiny scale tea farmers in Asia through education, direct trade, and creative partnerships.

I am driven and energized by a set of core values: allyship and advocacy, generosity, collective responsibility, and respect for others, their independence, and their capabilities. I have chosen to enter the field of web development because code is a language of action that can help me realize and activate these values in the form of software. I believe that software can dismantle all sorts of barriers, including those that restrict the access that we can gain to knowledge, to resources, and to our fellow humans.

My past experience has prepared me well for a career in web development. Many of the skills and experiences I have gained over the years translate very well into development: conceiving and realizing large-scale projects, working collaboratively, solving difficult problems, communicating across boundaries, and thinking creatively but in a structured, logical way, for example. In code, I have found a new language and a new passion. Having spent so much time thinking about media and digital technology from a theoretical and historical point of view, working hands on with code – a group of languages that do something – has been nothing short of thrilling. This move has felt like a natural next step in my career: I have shifted from a language of description (theory, analysis, scholarship) through a language that mixes description and action (strategic communications and policy development) and now into a language of pure action (code).

Having completed a 13-week, 500+ hour web development immersive with General Assembly, I am now seeking a professional situation where I can continue to work on challenging and meaningful projects with a team. I want to use code to help produce new possibilities for thinking, understanding, and living.
